Formal Puiseux Series

Manuel Eberl

17 February 2021

This is an unofficial reimagining of the Archive of Formal Proofs

Abstract

Formal Puiseux series are generalisations of formal power series and formal Laurent series that also allow for fractional exponents. They have the following general form: \[\sum_{i=N}^\infty a_{i/d} X^{i/d}\] where N is an integer and d is a positive integer.

This entry defines these series including their basic algebraic properties. Furthermore, it proves the Newtonâ€“Puiseux Theorem, namely that the Puiseux series over an algebraically closed field of characteristic 0 are also algebraically closed.
